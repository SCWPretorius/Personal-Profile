A quiet thing happened end of last year 2025. I got promoted to Technical Team Lead.

I've spent years buried in code, figuring out how systems should fit together, making things that don't break in six months, jumping on hard problems when the team needed it. Always enjoyed the people side too, unblocking folk, talking through designs and discussing the road map with stakeholders.

Then the AI push hit hard, business kept moving faster, and suddenly my job isn't mostly writing code anymore. It's stakeholder meetings, making sure what we're building actually matters to them, reviewing architecture so it doesn't turn into a mess later, pushing the team on practices that save us pain down the line and deciding what architecture is "good enough".

I still code... Personal stuff, quick prototypes to test ideas, spikes when something's stuck. That won't stop. But the day-to-day wins now come from different places; seeing a junior take full ownership of a big piece, turning a vague "make it better" ask into something concrete the team can run with, or just keeping everyone aligned so we ship without constant rework.

It's different. Sometimes I miss the flow state of just building. Other times it's satisfying in a new way, multiplying what the whole team can do instead of just my own output.
